Bengal Tiger

Name: Bengal Tiger
Family: Felidae
Habitat: Rainforests, Grasslands
Conservation Status: Endangered
Diet: Carnivores
Lifespan: 15 years

Conservation Status

The Bengal tiger is listed as endangered, with fewer than 2,500 individuals remaining in the wild.

Habitat

Bengal tigers primarily inhabit rainforests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps. They are found in India, Bangladesh, Bhutan, and Nepal.

Diet

They are carnivores and typically prey on large herbivores such as boar, and sometimes even buffalo.

Breeding

Bengal tigers usually give birth to 2-3 cubs after a gestation period of about 3.5 months.
