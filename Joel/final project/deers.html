About Deers

Deers are graceful animals known for their agility and beautiful antlers, often found in forests and grasslands around the world.

Deer

Name: Deer
Family: Cervidae
Habitat: Forests, Grasslands
Conservation Status: Varies (some endangered)

Conservation Status

While most deers species are not at immediate risk, some like Pere David's deer are endangered due to habitat loss and hunting. Conservation efforts have helped their numbers recover.

Habitat

Deers are typically found in a variety of habitats including forests, grasslands, and wetlands across multiple continents.

Diet

Deers are herbivores, feeding primarily on grasses, leaves, fruits, and nuts depending on their habitat and the season.

Breeding

Most deers species have distinct mating seasons known as "the rut," after which females give birth to one or two fawns following a gestation of about six to seven months.
